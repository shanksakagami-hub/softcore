Bachelor of Technology in Electrical and Electronic Engineering

Program Overview

The Bachelor of Technology in Electrical and Electronic Engineering is a four-year program designed to provide students with practical and theoretical knowledge in electrical engineering fundamentals, electronics, power systems, control systems, and communication technologies.

Program Objectives:

  • To provide students with hands-on skills in electrical and electronic engineering
  • To prepare graduates for technical careers in the electrical engineering industry
  • To equip students with problem-solving skills for real-world engineering challenges
  • To foster innovation and technological advancement in the field of electrical engineering

Curriculum Structure

Year 1: Semester 1

3 Units EE101: Introduction to Electrical Engineering

Basic principles of electrical engineering, circuit theory, and electrical measurements.

4 Units EE102: Engineering Mathematics I

Calculus, differential equations, and linear algebra for engineering applications.

3 Units EE103: Physics for Engineers

Mechanics, thermodynamics, and electromagnetism with engineering focus.

3 Units EE104: Introduction to Computer Programming

Programming fundamentals using C and Python for engineering problem-solving.

2 Units EE105: Communication Skills

Technical writing, presentation skills, and professional communication.

Year 1: Semester 2

3 Units EE106: Electric Circuit Analysis

DC and AC circuit analysis, network theorems, and circuit design.

4 Units EE107: Engineering Mathematics II

Advanced calculus, complex analysis, and differential equations.

3 Units EE108: Digital Logic Design

Boolean algebra, logic gates, combinational and sequential circuits.

3 Units EE109: Workshop Practice

Hands-on training in electrical workshop tools, safety, and basic fabrication.

2 Units EE110: Engineering Drawing

Technical drawing principles and computer-aided design basics.
